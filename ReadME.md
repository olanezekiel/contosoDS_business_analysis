# E-Commerce Sales & Customer Intelligence Analysis

## OVERVIEW

This project is an end-to-end SQL analysis of e-commerce customer behavior, focusing on lifetime value (LTV), cohort maturation, and retention dynamics. The objective of this analysis is to transition from purely tracking top-of-funnel acquisition to understanding long-term customer profitability. By segmenting the user base and identifying structural leaks in retention, this project provides data-driven strategies to maximize revenue and minimize churn.

---

## ANALYSIS APPROACH

### 1. Customer Segmentation

**Question:** Who are our most valuable customers?

**Analysis Approach:**
Calculated the total Lifetime Value (LTV) for every individual customer and utilized percentile distribution to categorize the user base into three distinct value tiers:

* **High-Value:** Top 25% of spenders.
* **Medium-Value:** Middle 50% of spenders.
* **Low-Value:** Bottom 25% of spenders.

**Query:**
[1_customer_segmentation](/1_customer_segmentation.sql)

**Visualization:**
![customer_segmentation](/images/1.png)


**Key Findings:**

* **Pareto Concentration:** The High-Value segment comprises exactly 25% of the user base but generates an overwhelming **$144.0M (65.6%)** of total revenue.
* **The Floor:** The lowest 25% of customers generate only $4.6M (2.1%), averaging an LTV of just $373 compared to the $11,638 average of the High-Value tier.

**Business Insight:**
Revenue is structurally dependent on a highly lucrative core. Losing even a fractional percentage of the "High-Value" tier will cause disproportionate financial damage. Conversely, the "Medium-Value" tier ($70.9M) represents the largest unoptimized growth vector for targeted upselling.

---

### 2. Cohort Analysis

**Question:** How do different customer groups generate long-term revenue?

**Analysis Approach:**
Grouped customers into cohorts based on their `first_purchase_date` to isolate and evaluate the average lifetime revenue generated by an individual customer within specific acquisition years.

**Query:**
[2_cohort_analysis.sql](/2_cohort_analysis.sql)

**Visualization:**
![cohort analysis](/images/2.png)


**Key Findings:**

* **Historical Peaks:** The 2016 and 2019 cohorts are the most valuable historically, consistently averaging over $3,000 in revenue per customer.
* **Volume vs. Value:** 2022 marked the highest year for sheer acquisition volume (9,010 customers), but the average value per customer dropped to $2,533.

**Business Insight:**
Recent marketing efforts have successfully driven top-of-funnel volume, but the actual quality and long-term spending habits of these new acquisitions are lagging behind historical peaks. We must investigate whether recent acquisition channels are optimizing for single-purchase buyers rather than long-term brand loyalists.

---

### 3. Retention Analysis

**Question:** Which customers haven't purchased recently?

**Analysis Approach:**
Utilized window functions to isolate the absolute most recent transaction for every customer. Categorized the base into 'Active' (purchase within the last 6 months) and 'Churned' (no purchase in over 6 months).

**Query:**
[3_Retention_Analysis.sql](/3_retention_analysis.sql)

**Visualization:**
![Retention_analysis](/images/3.png)

**Key Findings:**

* **Massive Lapsing:** Out of a historical base of 49,487 customers, an alarming **85.8% (42,472)** are currently classified as churned.
* **Active Base:** Only 7,015 customers remain actively engaged with the brand.
* Cross-referencing the churn timeline reveals the vast majority of lapsing occurred from the high-volume 2022 and 2023 cohorts.

**Business Insight:**
The business is operating with a severe "leaky bucket" retention model. While we can acquire users at scale, the post-purchase experience is failing to convert them into repeat buyers. The company is currently too reliant on expensive new customer acquisition to sustain revenue.

## STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATION

Based on the synthesis of segmentation, cohort, and retention data, the business must immediately pivot its primary focus from **Top-of-Funnel Acquisition** to **Mid-Funnel Retention and Monetization**.

1. **Deploy Aggressive Win-Back Campaigns:** We hold the data for over 22,000 customers acquired in 2022/2023 who have recently lapsed. Marketing should deploy targeted reactivation workflows (e.g., heavy discount ladders) to this specific segment. Re-engaging even 5% of this list will yield immediate revenue spikes.
2. **Launch a VIP Infrastructure:** Given that 65% of revenue is tied to just 25% of the user base, protecting this cohort is paramount. Implement white-glove customer service, early-access product drops, or a dedicated loyalty program strictly for this tier to bulletproof their retention.
3. **Audit Acquisition Quality:** The drop in average cohort value over recent years indicates our ad spend might be targeting the wrong demographics. Marketing must align their targeting parameters with the profiles of our historical 2016/2019 cohorts rather than optimizing purely for cheap conversion volume.
